FlashLive Child Safety Standards (CSAE)

Last updated: 2026-02-20

Zero-tolerance policy

FlashLive has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). Any content, behavior, or account associated with CSAE is strictly prohibited.

Prohibited content and behavior

  • Any sexual content involving minors.
  • Grooming, solicitation, coercion, or exploitation of minors.
  • Sharing, requesting, promoting, or distributing child sexual abuse material (CSAM).
  • Any attempt to facilitate abuse, trafficking, or endangerment of minors.

In-app reporting

Users can report safety concerns directly in-app using the Report Concern button during chat sessions. Reports are logged for moderation review.

Automated content moderation

FlashLive uses automated AI-based nudity and explicit content detection on live video streams. When explicit content is detected on a user's camera feed:

  • The session is terminated immediately and automatically.
  • The account is permanently banned from the platform.
  • The violation is logged with a timestamp and technical details for moderation review.

This system operates in addition to manual reporting and does not replace user responsibility to follow community standards.

Enforcement

  • Immediate account/session action for suspected CSAE behavior.
  • Automated detection and instant ban for explicit content shown on camera.
  • Preservation of relevant report and technical records as required by law.
  • Cooperation with lawful requests from appropriate authorities.

Legal compliance and authority reporting

FlashLive complies with relevant child safety laws and reports to regional and national authorities when required by applicable law.

Contact

For urgent child safety matters related to FlashLive, contact: safety@flashlive.app
